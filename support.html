Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Find quick answers to the most common questions about Krishna Journal.

Getting started is simple! Download the app from the App Store or Google Play (links below), create a free account with your email address, and you're ready to begin. On your first launch, you'll be guided through a short onboarding that sets your preferred language (Hindi or English) and lets you configure daily reminder times. Then just tap "New Entry," describe how you're feeling, and receive your first personalized shloka from the Bhagavad Gita. No prior knowledge of the Gita is needed — the app meets you exactly where you are.

Absolutely. Your privacy is our highest priority. All journal entries are encrypted using AES-256 encryption both in transit and at rest. We do not read, share, or sell your personal journal content. The only time your text leaves the device is to securely sync across your own devices or to generate your shloka recommendation via our AI service — and even then, it is never stored by our AI provider beyond the immediate request. You can review our full Privacy Policy for complete details, and you can delete your account and all associated data at any time from Settings → Account → Delete Account.

When you write a journal entry, Krishna Journal sends your text to an AI model (powered by Anthropic's Claude) along with our carefully crafted spiritual context. The AI identifies the core emotional theme of your entry — whether it's anxiety, grief, confusion, ambition, gratitude, or something else entirely — and maps it to the most relevant verse from the 700 shlokas of the Bhagavad Gita. It then provides a thoughtful, personalized explanation of how that verse applies to your situation. The AI is instructed to respond with the wisdom and compassion of Lord Krishna's teachings, not as a generic assistant. Results are delivered within a few seconds.

Yes! Full Hindi language support is available for Premium subscribers. This includes the entire app interface, shloka text with Devanagari script, transliteration, and AI-generated explanations all in Hindi. You can switch languages at any time from Settings → Language. Free users have access to English only. If you write your journal entries in Hindi, the AI will understand your entry and respond in Hindi as well. We believe the Gita's wisdom should be accessible in the language that feels most natural and sacred to you.

You can upgrade to Premium directly within the app. Go to Settings → Upgrade to Premium, or tap the "Unlock Premium" banner that appears when you reach your daily free limits. Premium is available as a monthly subscription ($4.99/month) or an annual plan ($35.99/year — a 40% saving). Payment is handled securely through the Apple App Store or Google Play, so we never see your card details. Your Premium benefits activate immediately after purchase and sync across all your devices. You can cancel your subscription at any time through your App Store or Play Store account settings — you'll retain Premium access until the end of your billing period.

We truly appreciate bug reports — they help us make the app better for everyone! The quickest way to report a bug is through the in-app feedback tool: go to Settings → Send Feedback → Report a Bug. This automatically attaches your device model, OS version, and app version (no personal data) so we can reproduce the issue faster. Alternatively, fill in the contact form below with a description of what happened, the steps to reproduce it, and your device model and OS version. We aim to acknowledge all bug reports within 24 hours and release fixes in our next update.

Yes, Premium subscribers can export all their journal entries as a PDF or plain-text file from Settings → Export Journal. This gives you a beautiful, formatted printout of your reflections and the shlokas you received — a wonderful keepsake of your spiritual journey. Your entries are also automatically synced to our secure servers so they're backed up across your devices in real time. If you switch phones or reinstall the app, simply sign in to your account and all your entries will be restored.

AI is powerful but not perfect, and spiritual guidance is deeply personal. If a shloka or explanation doesn't resonate with you, you can tap "Refresh Guidance" on any entry to receive a different shloka. You can also tap "Give Feedback" to let us know the response missed the mark — this helps us continuously improve our AI prompt tuning. Remember, the Bhagavad Gita contains 700 verses across 18 chapters, and different verses may speak to you at different times. We encourage you to sit with the shloka for a moment before dismissing it — sometimes the wisdom becomes clearer on second reading.
